Public Universities In Kenya

Before December 13, 2012, public universities were created by the individual Acts of Parliament. After the promulgation of the Universities Law No. 42 of 2012, state universities have since been established by granting the charter. All individual laws were repealed and previous public universities were re-accredited by awarding laws after reviewing institutional quality.

Below is the list of some public universities that have been fully accredited as of 30th June 2013.

  • University of Nairobi (UoN) – established 1970 and chartered 2013
  • Moi University (MU) – established 1984 and chartered 2013
  • Kenyatta University (KU) – established 1985 and chartered 2013
  • Egerton University (EU) – established 1987 and chartered 2013
  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT) – established 1994 and chartered 2013

More information on the top Universities in Kenya,

University of Nairobi

Established in 1970, the University of Nairobi is a non-profit public higher education institution located in the urban setting of the large city of Nairobi (population range of 1,000,000-5,000,000 inhabitants). This institution has branch campuses in the following location(s): Kikuyu, Parklands, Lower Kabete, Upper Kabete, Chiromo, Kisumu. Officially accredited and/or recognized by the Commission for University Education, Kenya, the University of Nairobi (UoN) is a very large (uniRank enrollment range: over-45,000 students) coeducational higher education institution.

Kenyatta University

Kenyatta University is one of the best universities in Kenya and also a non-profit public higher education institution located in the suburban setting of the large city of Nairobi (population range of 1,000,000-5,000,000 inhabitants). This institution has branch campuses in the following location(s): Mombasa, Kitui, Nakuru, Embu, Ruiru, Nyeri, Kiambu, Kericho, Dadaab. Officially accredited and/or recognized by the Commission for University Education, Kenya, Kenyatta University (KU) is a very large (uniRank enrollment range: over-45,000 students) coeducational higher education institution. Kenyatta University (KU) also offers courses and programs leading to officially recognized higher education degrees such as pre-bachelor degrees (i.e. certificates, diplomas, associate or foundation degrees), bachelor degrees, master degrees, doctorate degrees in several areas of study.

Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology

Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology is a non-profit public educational institution located in the suburban environment of the great city of Nairobi (population between 1,000,000-5,000,000). JKUAT also offers courses and programs that lead to officially recognized degrees, such as pre-bachelor degrees (i.e certificates, diplomas, associates, or foundation certificates), bachelor’s, masters, and Ph.D. degrees in several fields.

Strathmore University

Strathmore University is known as one of the best universities in Kenya and also a non-profit private higher education institution located in the urban setting of the large city of Nairobi (population range of 1,000,000-5,000,000 inhabitants). Officially accredited and/or recognized by the Commission for University Education, Kenya, Strathmore University (SU) is a small (uniRank enrollment range: 4,000-4,999 students) coeducational higher education institution. This 18-year-old higher education institution has a selective admission policy based on entrance tests, academic history, and students’ past grades. The admission rate ranges between 30 and 40%, which makes this Kenyan Higher Education Institution very selective. International students are eligible for registration.

Moi University

Moi University, founded in 1984, is a public, non-profit, educational institution located in the suburban area of the large city of Eldoret (with a population of 50,000- 249,999 people), Uasin Gishu. This institution has also branch campuses in the following location(s): Eldoret West Campus, Nairobi, Kitale, Kericho, Odero Akang’o. Officially accredited and/or recognized by the Commission for University Education, Kenya, Moi University (MU) is a very large (uniRank enrollment range: 30,000-34,999 students) coeducational higher education institution.

FAQS

Is education free in Kenya?

Primary education is free and compulsory in Kenya. Secondary education is also free, but it is not compulsory. Primary education lasts 8 years in Kenya (standards 1 to 8). Students are usually 6 years old when they start school and 14 years old upon completion of their primary education.

Does Kenya have good education?

Since 1985, general education in Kenya is based on a system of 8 to 4 years, with eight years of primary education followed by four years of high school and four years of college or university. There are a large number of public and private universities in the country, as well as middle-level colleges
